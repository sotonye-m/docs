## Overview

The Yativo Card Issuer Program lets businesses issue virtual and physical payment cards to their own customers under their platform. As an issuer you manage the full card lifecycle — from customer onboarding to card creation, balance funding, and transaction monitoring — through a single API.

**Typical use cases:**
- Fintech apps offering spending cards to users
- Corporate expense management platforms
- Digital banking services for underserved markets
- Wallets that want to add a card spending layer

<Note>
  Access to the Card Issuer Program is by invitation only. Contact your Yativo account manager or [support@yativo.com](mailto:support@yativo.com) to request access. The program section will not appear in your dashboard until your account manager enables it for your account.
</Note>

---

## How It Works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Your account manager grants eligibility">
    Yativo's team reviews your use case and enables the Card Issuer Program on your account. Nothing is visible in your dashboard until this step is complete.
  </Step>
  <Step title="You submit an application">
    Once eligible, the Card Issuer section appears in your dashboard. Choose your funding network (SOL or XDC) and an optional IBAN add-on, then submit.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Yativo approves your program">
    Our compliance team reviews and approves the application, typically within 2–5 business days. You will receive an email confirmation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fund your master wallet and issue cards">
    Deposit to your master wallet, onboard customers, issue cards, and fund them by customer email, ID, or your own reference.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Apply for the Program

Once your account manager has granted eligibility, submit your application.

```
POST /card-issuer/apply
```

<ParamField body="funding_structure" type="string" required>
  The blockchain network your master wallet will use:
  - `master_wallet` — SOL-based (USDC on Solana, ~2–5 s finality)
  - `non_master` — XDC-based (USDC on XDC Network, ~2 s finality, near-zero fees)
</ParamField>

<ParamField body="iban_enabled" type="boolean">
  Set to `true` to enable EUR IBAN issuance for your customers. Requires additional regulatory review. Default: `false`.
</ParamField>

<RequestExample>
```bash cURL
curl -X POST 'https://crypto-api.yativo.com/api/v1/card-issuer/apply' \
  -H 'Authorization: Bearer YOUR_TOKEN' \
  -H 'Content-Type: application/json' \
  -d '{
    "funding_structure": "master_wallet",
    "iban_enabled": false
  }'
```

```typescript TypeScript
const result = await sdk.cardIssuer.apply({
  funding_structure: "master_wallet",
  iban_enabled: false,
});
```
</RequestExample>

<ResponseExample>
```json Success
{
  "success": true,
  "message": "Application submitted. We will be in touch within 2–5 business days.",
  "data": {
    "program_id": "iss_01HX9KZMB3F7VNQP8R2WDGT111",
    "status": "pending",
    "funding_structure": "master_wallet",
    "iban_enabled": false,
    "created_at": "2026-04-01T10:00:00Z"
  }
}
```

```json Not eligible (403)
{
  "success": false,
  "error": "NOT_ELIGIBLE",
  "message": "You are not eligible for the Card Issuer Program. Contact your account manager to request access."
}
```
</ResponseExample>

## Master Wallet & Funding

### Networks

Your program runs on one of two networks, chosen at application time:

| Option | Network | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| `master_wallet` | Solana | High volume, consumer payments |
| `non_master` | XDC Network | Enterprise, near-zero fee environments |

### Currency Balances

Your master wallet holds three spendable currency balances:

| Balance | Currency | How to add funds |
|---|---|---|
| USD | US Dollars | Send USDC to your master wallet address |
| EUR | Euros | Send EURe to your master wallet address |
| GBP | British Pounds | Send GBPe to your master wallet address |

<Note>
  Each deposit token maps to the matching fiat currency — USDC goes to your USD balance, EURe to EUR, GBPe to GBP. Settlement is handled automatically. You and your customers only ever see USD, EUR, and GBP balances.
</Note>

### Depositing Funds

Send the appropriate token to your master wallet deposit address (shown in your dashboard and in the `GET /card-issuer/wallets` response):

- **To add USD balance** — send USDC to your SOL or XDC deposit address
- **To add EUR balance** — send EURe to your deposit address
- **To add GBP balance** — send GBPe to your deposit address

<Tip>
  Always copy your deposit address from the dashboard or API — never type it manually. Deposits to the wrong address cannot be recovered.
</Tip>

## Customer Management

### Onboard a Customer

Creates the customer's card account and begins their KYC flow.

```
POST /yativo-card/onboard
```

<ParamField body="email" type="string" required>Email address.</ParamField>
<ParamField body="first_name" type="string" required>First name.</ParamField>
<ParamField body="last_name" type="string" required>Last name.</ParamField>
<ParamField body="date_of_birth" type="string" required>Format: `YYYY-MM-DD`.</ParamField>
<ParamField body="country" type="string" required>ISO 3166-1 alpha-2 country code.</ParamField>
<ParamField body="external_reference" type="string">
  Your own user/customer ID. Can be used later to look up or fund this customer.
</ParamField>

<RequestExample>
```bash cURL
curl -X POST 'https://crypto-api.yativo.com/api/v1/yativo-card/onboard' \
  -H 'Authorization: Bearer YOUR_TOKEN' \
  -H 'Content-Type: application/json' \
  -d '{
    "email": "customer@example.com",
    "first_name": "Jane",
    "last_name": "Doe",
    "date_of_birth": "1990-05-15",
    "country": "US",
    "external_reference": "user_9821"
  }'
```
</RequestExample>

<ResponseExample>
```json Success
{
  "success": true,
  "data": {
    "yativo_card_id": "yc_01HXABC123",
    "email": "customer@example.com",
    "kyc_status": "pending",
    "card_wallet_deployed": false
  }
}
```
</ResponseExample>

## Fund a Customer's Card

Transfer funds from your master wallet balance to a customer's card. Identify the customer by **email address**, **Yativo card ID**, or your own **external reference**.

```
POST /card-issuer/fund
```

<ParamField body="identifier" type="string" required>
  The customer's email address, Yativo card ID (`yc_...`), or `external_reference`.
</ParamField>

<ParamField body="amount" type="number" required>
  Amount to fund.
</ParamField>

<ParamField body="currency" type="string" required>
  Currency of the amount. Accepted values: `USD`, `EUR`, `GBP`.
</ParamField>

<RequestExample>
```bash cURL — fund by email
curl -X POST 'https://crypto-api.yativo.com/api/v1/card-issuer/fund' \
  -H 'Authorization: Bearer YOUR_TOKEN' \
  -H 'Content-Type: application/json' \
  -d '{
    "identifier": "customer@example.com",
    "amount": 100.00,
    "currency": "USD"
  }'
```

```bash cURL — fund by external reference
curl -X POST 'https://crypto-api.yativo.com/api/v1/card-issuer/fund' \
  -H 'Authorization: Bearer YOUR_TOKEN' \
  -H 'Content-Type: application/json' \
  -d '{
    "identifier": "user_9821",
    "amount": 75.00,
    "currency": "GBP"
  }'
```

```typescript TypeScript
// Fund by email
await sdk.cardIssuer.fundCustomer({
  identifier: "customer@example.com",
  amount: 100.00,
  currency: "USD",
});

// Fund by your own user ID
await sdk.cardIssuer.fundCustomer({
  identifier: "user_9821",
  amount: 75.00,
  currency: "GBP",
});
```
</RequestExample>

<ResponseExample>
```json Success
{
  "success": true,
  "data": {
    "funding_id": "fund_01HX9ABC",
    "customer_id": "yc_01HXABC123",
    "amount": 100.00,
    "currency": "USD",
    "status": "completed",
    "funded_at": "2026-04-16T10:30:00Z"
  }
}
```

```json Insufficient balance (402)
{
  "success": false,
  "error": "INSUFFICIENT_BALANCE",
  "message": "Insufficient USD balance in your master wallet. Current balance: $32.50"
}
```
</ResponseExample>

## Issue a Card for a Customer

Once a customer's KYC is approved and their card wallet is deployed, issue a virtual or physical card.

```
POST /yativo-card/customers/{yativoCardId}/create-card
```

<ParamField path="yativoCardId" type="string" required>
  The customer's Yativo card account ID (`yc_...`).
</ParamField>

<ParamField body="card_type" type="string" required>
  `virtual` or `physical`.
</ParamField>

<ParamField body="display_name" type="string">
  A label for the card shown in the dashboard.
</ParamField>

<RequestExample>
```bash cURL
curl -X POST 'https://crypto-api.yativo.com/api/v1/yativo-card/customers/yc_01HXABC123/create-card' \
  -H 'Authorization: Bearer YOUR_TOKEN' \
  -H 'Content-Type: application/json' \
  -d '{
    "card_type": "virtual",
    "display_name": "Expense Card"
  }'
```
</RequestExample>

## Webhooks

Subscribe to issuer events using the [Webhooks API](/yativo-crypto/webhooks):

| Event | Description |
|---|---|
| `issuer.program.approved` | Your program application was approved |
| `issuer.customer.kyc_approved` | A customer's KYC was approved |
| `issuer.customer.card_created` | A card was issued for a customer |
| `issuer.funding.completed` | A card funding settled successfully |
| `issuer.funding.failed` | A card funding attempt failed |
